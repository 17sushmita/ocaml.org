Books

OCaml currently suffers from a lack of paperback books. New books are on the way, but in the meantime, you can easily find up-to-date books on OCaml online here.

Online Books on OCaml

  • Introduction to OCaml by J. Hickey (PDF [cached]).

    This book is notoriously much more than just an introduction to OCaml, it describes most of the language, and is accessible.

  • Developing Applications With OCaml by E. Chailloux, P. Manoury and B. Pagano (Online, PDF [cached]).

    This book is a translation of a French book published by OReilly. It does not only cover the language, but also the runtime and the tools, whose understanding is necessary to actually develop a complete project in OCaml.

  • Unix system programming in OCaml by Xavier Leroy and Didier RÃ©my (Online).

    This is an excellent book on Unix system programming, with an emphasis on communications between processes. The main novelty of this work is the use of OCaml, instead of the C language that is customary in systems programming. This gives an unusual perspective on systems programming and on OCaml.

  • Using, Understanding, and Unraveling OCaml by D. Remy (Online, PDF [cached]).

    This book describes both the OCaml language and the theoretical grounds behind its powerful type system. A good complement to other books on OCaml.

  • How to Think Like a Computer Scientist by Nicholas Monje and Allen Downey (Online).
